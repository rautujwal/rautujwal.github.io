Insurance Portfolio Review Prioritisation

The problem

A portfolio of 1.05M insurance records with no structured way to prioritise which policies were worth investigating first. Review effort was spread equally across everything - expensive and ineffective.

The approach

Built an 8-step SQL workflow that worked through baseline benchmarking, lift scoring, Pareto concentration analysis, tenure cohort comparisons and segment stability checks - systematically ruling things out until the real concentration revealed itself at the policy level.

The findings
Top 30% of policies generated 55% of total claims across the 1.05M record portfolio
  • Segment-level analysis (policy type × location) showed balanced behaviour - claim share tracked premium share with no meaningful lift across any segment
  • Tenure cohort analysis confirmed claim intensity remained nearly constant across policy age groups - lifecycle stage was not a driver
  • Concentration surfaced at the individual policy level via decile analysis - top two deciles alone accounted for 45% of total claims
  • Stability checks confirmed segment metrics were structurally reliable - not statistical noise
  • Workflow is modular and rerunnable on future portfolio snapshots without rebuilding from scratch

Used Vehicle Pricing Review Tool

The problem

The used car market is volatile by nature. Price depreciation depends on mileage, age, fuel type, transmission and more - making it genuinely difficult to know what a fair price looks like. Most buyers and sellers have no reliable way to check before committing to a deal.

The approach

Built a pricing decision support tool across 8,128 used vehicle listings. Users enter their vehicle details and the tool returns an expected market price, benchmarks their price against comparable listings, and delivers a plain-language pricing summary. A simulation layer lets buyers and sellers adjust their price and instantly see how market positioning changes, before going to market.

The findings
  • Used vehicle listings showed consistent and explainable price variation driven mainly by age and mileage, allowing listings with significant deviation from expected pricing to be systematically flagged for review
  • The system fulfils its functional requirements by providing the expected price output, Z-score risk scoring and a meaningful recommendation to guide both buyers, sellers and the pricing review team

Tableau · Python · Labour Market

Australian Labour Market Pressure Analysis

The problem

Early-career job seekers have no structured way to assess which occupations and states have genuine workforce shortages - or whether graduate supply is already closing them. The market feels noisy without a data reference point.

The approach

Drew on three datasets - Internet Vacancy Index (IVI) job ads, ABS labour force statistics, and 485 Graduate Visa inflows - to examine demand and supply conditions across occupations, states and time. Python was used to clean, standardise and merge the data into a unified structure for consistent comparison across segments. The processed dataset was then brought into Tableau to analyse pressure trends and build a visual story comparing demand, supply and graduate inflows across occupation groups and states from 2020 to 2025.

The findings
2022 peak pressure year - demand grew faster than supply across most occupation groups
  • Labour demand (IVI job ads) was the primary driver of workforce pressure changes, not shifts in labour supply
  • Labour market tightness peaked around 2022 and eased afterward, but remained above pre-2020 levels across most occupations
  • Inflows of 485 Graduate Visas did not show a consistent or direct impact on easing occupation-level shortages
  • Some occupations improved despite stable or rising graduate inflows, indicating demand cooling rather than supply adjustment
  • Labour shortages were concentrated in specific occupations and states rather than evenly distributed across the economy
